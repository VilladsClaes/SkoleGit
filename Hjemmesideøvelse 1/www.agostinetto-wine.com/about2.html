    Maremma

    This is a unique area for wine production, with really diverse climates and terroirs. Sangiovese, the most common variety in Tuscany, represents less than half of all the hectares you can find in Maremma, leaving significant room for traditional varieties such as Ciliegiolo, Vermentino and Trebbiano and for international ones such as Sauvignon, Syrah and Cabernet giving a diversity that has no comparison with other regions in Tuscany.

The begin

Our Story

It was at the end of XVIII that NOME BISNONNO started a small production in Valdobbiadene, home of the Prosecco di Valdobbiadene DOCG, getting recognition for the quality of his product during the interwar period.

Later on, his son NOME NONNO followed up on his father’s footsteps moving south in the region settling in Montebelluna, producing Prosecco di Asolo e Montello DOCG from a marvellous vineyard overlooking the surroundings from Euganean Hills to Venice.

Today

Our Present

It was after a family trip in Tuscany region of Maremma that we fell in love with an estate in the village of Montiano, home of Morellino, overlooking the Tyrrhenian Sea and Isola del Giglio.

We loved it so much we bought it, starting with grafting a 10 hectares vineyard with Sangiovese, Ciliegiolo and Vermentino grapes in the early and we followed up buying a small winery nearby. We know produce Morellino di Scansano DOCG, Vermentino di Maremma Toscana DOCG and Ciliegiolo.
